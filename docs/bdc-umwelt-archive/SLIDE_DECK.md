## Slide 2 — The problem (ecology + culture)

- Extinction removes **species** and **ecological function**  
- It also erodes **shared memory**: shifting baselines, archival bias, **hype cycles** (de-extinction as spectacle)  
- We lose not only bodies but **temporal coexistence**: rhythms, migrations, synchrony  

**Speaker note:** Bridge to **human responsibility** without claiming neuroscience of “memory erasure.”

---

## Slide 3 — Design question

- How do we **translate** “extinction–absence” into **perceivable, discussable** experience?  
- How can **AI + biological evidence** help **re-build a disciplined public memory**—without faking nature?  
- How do we end in **accountability**, not only immersion?  

**Speaker note:** Three questions = three acts.

## Slide 4 — Concept (one line)

- **Umwelt Archive** — a planetary dossier system where **geo-evidence** becomes **constrained sensation + rhythm**, and every generative scene carries **citations + confidence**  

**Speaker note:** “Umwelt” = situated perception; we only simulate **within priors**.

## Slide 6 — Act I: Amnesia mechanics (interactive)

**Speaker note:** This is the **critical difference** from generic AI panoramas.
